## The Neon Jacket Wasp (*Vespula luminescens*)

The **Neon Jacket** is a highly evolved, bioluminescent relative of the common yellow jacket. Native to dense, nocturnal rainforests and increasingly found adapting to the shadowy alleys of sprawling cyberpunk metropolises, this fictional insect is as mesmerizing as it is dangerous. 

---

### Physical Appearance

At first glance, the Neon Jacket shares the sleek, aerodynamic build of a standard wasp, but its coloring sets it entirely apart. 

* **Bioluminescent Exoskeleton:** Instead of the typical yellow and black banding, the Neon Jacket features deep, matte-black chitin striped with vivid, glowing bands of **cyan, magenta, or acid green**.
* **Pulsing Abdomen:** The intensity of their glow is tied to their agitation levels. A calm worker wasp emits a soft, rhythmic pulse, while an angry swarmer blazes with a harsh, strobe-like intensity.
* **Wings:** Their wings are transparent but possess an iridescent, oil-slick sheen that catches and scatters their own body light during flight, leaving faint trails of light in the air.

### Habitat and Nesting

Neon Jackets are master architects of the dark. They build intricate paper nests, but their building materials are far from ordinary.

* **Glowing Hives:** In urban environments, they chew up discarded synthetic materials, synthetic polymers, and neon-dyed trash, mixing it with their specialized saliva. The resulting nest resembles a glowing, pulsing paper lantern clinging to the underside of fire escapes or hidden deep within abandoned subway shafts.
* **Thermal Preferences:** They thrive in warm, humid environments, often nesting near exhaust vents or glowing server farms to absorb the ambient heat.

### Diet

To maintain their incredible luminescence, Neon Jackets require a highly specialized diet.

* **Luminescent Flora:** In their natural forest habitats, they feed on the nectar of rare bioluminescent fungi and night-blooming orchids.
* **Urban Foraging:** In cities, they have adapted to scavenge energy-rich, synthetic sugars found in spilled energy drinks, chemical runoff, and discarded battery acid, which fuels the chemical reaction in their glowing abdomens.

### The Sting: The "Blacklight Burn"

Like their non-glowing cousins, Neon Jackets are fiercely territorial, but their sting carries a unique and lingering effect. 

* **Venom Properties:** The venom contains a phototoxic enzyme. When injected, it causes a sharp, burning pain.
* **The Aftermath:** The skin around the sting site reacts to the venom by temporarily glowing under ultraviolet light—a phenomenon locals call the **"blacklight burn."** The glowing bruise can last for up to a week, serving as a glaring warning to others to stay away from the hive.
